Joker

First Appearance: Batman #1 (1940) Overview: The Clown Prince of Crime is Batman's arch-nemesis. Known for his chaotic nature and dark humor, the Joker embodies anarchy and madness. His unpredictable behavior and cunning intellect make him a constant threat.

Penguin

First Appearance: Detective Comics #58 (1941) Overview: Oswald Cobblepot, known as the Penguin, is a criminal mastermind with a penchant for elegance. Often depicted as a mob boss, he uses his cunning and resources to control Gotham's underworld.

Two-Face

First Appearance: Detective Comics #66 (1942) Overview: Once Gotham’s district attorney, Harvey Dent becomes Two-Face after a tragic accident scars half his face. His duality symbolizes the conflict between good and evil, as he makes decisions based on the flip of a coin.

Scarecrow

First Appearance: World’s Finest Comics #3 (1941) Overview: Dr. Jonathan Crane, known as Scarecrow, uses fear as his weapon. His expertise in psychology allows him to manipulate his victims' phobias, making him a chilling adversary for Batman.

Bane

First Appearance: Batman: Vengeance of Bane #1 (1993) Overview: Bane is known for his physical strength and intellect. His goal to destroy Batman leads to one of the most iconic storylines, "Knightfall," where he famously breaks the Dark Knight’s back.

Poison Ivy

First Appearance: Batman #181 (1966) Overview: Pamela Isley, or Poison Ivy, is an eco-terrorist with the ability to control plants. Her motives often stem from a desire to protect nature, making her a complex villain who occasionally allies with Batman against greater threats.
